Every month, a small-business owner closes their books. They have a bank statement, payroll files, invoices, and receipts — each a different part of the same month. To know their true position, they correlate it all by hand. Every month.

Archon does that correlation for you. Watch. On the dashboard, you drop your documents straight onto the run ledger, and eight agents go to work in sequence. The Extractor reads each document live with AWS Bedrock vision. Then the engine classifies it, links the related documents into a single financial event, validates them against each other, and recomputes the close — and the affected tiles flash and update in front of you. Upload, correlate, done.

Take a single payroll event. It produces three documents that each tell only part of the story. The bank confirmation shows the net amount that left the account. The payroll register holds the full employer cost — gross pay plus employer social-security contributions. On the books of our demo company, January twenty twenty-six, the bank transfer totals three thousand nine hundred ninety-five euros, but the true employer cost is six thousand nine hundred thirty. That difference — two thousand nine hundred thirty-five euros — is the social-security and tax wedge. Not a scandal: it is only visible because Archon correlated the register with the bank transfer.

So Archon tells you one of two things. Either every document type is present and linked — your books are complete and reconciled. Or: the register shows a higher cost than the bank transferred — are you missing a document? And you can search everything you have uploaded — find any document, vendor, or person in milliseconds.

Under the hood: the AI reads, a deterministic engine computes and checks completeness. Bedrock vision, Claude Sonnet four-point-six, reads each document at ninety-six point seven percent field accuracy on our labelled corpus; then deterministic rules do the math, so every number is reproducible and auditable. It runs on the zero stack — Next.js on Vercel, with AWS DynamoDB as the source of truth, an OpenSearch search read-model, and Terraform across the whole data tier.

We ran Archon on our own books at Reflective IKE. It pulled together the bank, payroll, and invoices and told us in seconds that everything reconciled — something that used to take our accountant the better part of a day. Try it live.
