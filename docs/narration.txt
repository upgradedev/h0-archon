Every month, a small-business owner closes their books. They have a bank statement, payroll files, invoices, and receipts — each a different part of the same month. To know their true position, they correlate it all by hand. Every month.

Archon is a document-collection and auto-correlation engine. You upload everything your business receives — purchases, sales, payments, receipts, payroll — and it links the related documents into single financial events and tells you whether your books are complete and reconciled.

Take a single payroll event. It produces three documents that each tell only part of the story. The bank confirmation shows the net amount that left the account. The payroll register holds the full employer cost — gross pay plus employer social-security. On the books of our demo company, ARCHON DEMO IKE, January twenty twenty-six, the bank transfer totals three thousand nine hundred ninety-five euros, but the true employer cost is six thousand nine hundred thirty. That difference — two thousand nine hundred thirty-five euros, about forty-two percent of the true cost — is the normal employer-IKA and tax wedge. Not a scandal — it is only visible because Archon correlated the register with the bank transfer.

So Archon voices one of two outcomes. Either every document type is present and linked — your books are complete. Or: the register shows a higher cost than the bank transferred — are you missing a document? For this close, everything reconciles: revenue of forty-seven thousand two hundred euros, EBITDA of thirty thousand six hundred ninety-eight.

Archon runs on the zero stack: Next.js on Vercel, AWS DynamoDB, and AWS Bedrock. The AI reads, a deterministic engine computes and checks completeness. Bedrock vision, running Claude Sonnet four-point-six, reads each document — ninety-six point seven percent field accuracy on our labelled eval corpus — then deterministic rules do the math, so every number is reproducible and auditable. DynamoDB is the source of truth: a single-table design where every read is a key-based lookup that scales to zero for stateless Vercel functions, plus an OpenSearch read-model and Terraform across the whole data tier.

We ran Archon on our own books at Reflective IKE. It pulled together the bank, payroll, and invoices and told us in seconds that everything reconciled — something that used to take our accountant the better part of a day. Try it live.
