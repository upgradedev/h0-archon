Every month, a small-business owner closes their books. They have a bank statement, payroll documents, invoices, and receipts — each telling a different version of the same month, with no obvious way to connect them. To know their true position, they correlate it all by hand. Every month.

Here is the core problem. One payroll event produces three documents that each tell only part of the story. The bank statement shows only the net amounts that left the account. The payroll register holds the full employer cost — gross pay plus employer social-security contributions — the number that belongs in your books. The payslips break it down per employee. You cannot match them by amount alone, so most businesses just record the bank line, and their profit and loss understates what payroll really cost.

On our sample books, the bank shows five thousand nine hundred fifty-seven euros going to staff. The true employer cost is nine thousand one hundred eleven. That gap never appears on the bank confirmation. Across our test corpus, bank-only bookkeeping understated true cost by three hundred fourteen thousand euros.

Archon fixes this. You upload your documents, and a pipeline of agents reads each one, links the related documents into a single event, validates them, and reconciles the numbers. The Event Linker recognises that this bank transfer, this register, and these payslips are the same event, and reports the true figure. What took an accountant hours now takes seconds.

Archon runs on the zero stack: Next.js on Vercel, with AWS DynamoDB and AWS Bedrock. The principle is simple — the AI reads, a deterministic engine computes. AWS Bedrock vision, running Claude Sonnet four-point-six, reads the scanned documents; we measured ninety-six point seven percent field accuracy. Then deterministic rules do the math, so every number is reproducible and auditable.

The dashboard is live on AWS DynamoDB: revenue net of tax, true employer cost down to each employee, multi-period trends, and customer and supplier statements — and here is that exact data in the evidence API.
